## Overview

This project is a comprehensive equity research report on Sterling Infrastructure, Inc. (NASDAQ: STRL), prepared for the **Stuart Investment Club** at Illinois Institute of Technology's Stuart School of Business. The analysis triangulates multiple valuation methodologies — five-year ratio analysis, Altman Z-Score bankruptcy scoring, DuPont ROE decomposition, and scenario-based P/E valuation — to arrive at a contrarian **NOT BUY** recommendation.

The core thesis: **Sterling Infrastructure is a high-quality business at a stretched price.** After a 113% rally from ~$177 (Dec 2024) to $377.84 (Nov 2025), the trailing P/E has expanded to ~45× — more than double its FY2024 exit multiple — while reported earnings were flattered by a $91M non-recurring gain on deconsolidation of a subsidiary.

## Investment Thesis

### Why NOT BUY at current levels

1. **Valuation has run ahead of fundamentals.** Trailing P/E of ~45× sits well above the 10–21× range STRL has historically traded in. The market is pricing in aggressive forward growth with no margin of safety.
2. **Earnings quality overstated by a one-time gain.** FY2024 net income of $258M includes a $91M gain on deconsolidation. Adjusted "clean" net income is ~$167M; adjusted growth moderates from +85.7% to ~+20%.
3. **Asymmetric risk/reward.** Bull case implies +14% upside; bear case implies −32% downside. Probability-weighted return is negative.

### What supports the quality of the business

- Gross margin expansion: 14.6% → 20.1% (FY2020–FY2024)
- Debt-to-Equity reduction: 2.55× → 1.44×
- $664M cash position (+40.9% YoY)
- $2.28B record backlog with ~70% convertible within 24 months
- E-Infrastructure segment operating margin: 22%

## Methodology

The valuation employs a multi-method framework:

- **Ratio Analysis (FY2020–FY2024):** Liquidity, Leverage, Efficiency, Profitability, and Market Value ratios across a five-year window.
- **Altman Z-Score:** Composite bankruptcy-risk measure — STRL scored 5.01, deep in the Safe Zone.
- **DuPont ROE Decomposition (3-step):** ROE decomposed into Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier.
- **Relative Valuation:** Peer-benchmarked P/E multiple applied to forward EPS estimate (adjusted for one-time gain).
- **DCF Cross-Check:** WACC range of 8.5%–10.5% with 2.5% terminal growth; fair value range of $320–$365.
- **Scenario Analysis:** Bull / Base / Bear cases spanning $256 to $432 implied price range.
